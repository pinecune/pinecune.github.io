The GhettoBox 420
The Story

After assembling my new desktop PC, I felt bad about decomissioning my old one. I decided to gut some old components from it and make a portable PC that I could take to LAN parties. Unfortunately, no cases on the market satisfied my vision of small and those cases that came close were out of my budget. Fortunately, I had access to a workshop where I had the tools to make my own. I started building during the very relaxing period of Finals Week and spent only about $25, 17 man hours, and one letter grade to bring it to completion. Finals period can't melt steel dreams.

The Result

I wish I had more pictures of the design and manufacturing process, but as it was finals period, I didn't spend any time to record my struggle. Instead, I have a few intermediate progress pictures. I've uploaded them into an album below. For what it's worth, I'm happy with the product. It does what I need it to do, which is play DotA2 and store movies.
